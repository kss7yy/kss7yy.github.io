Who am I?

Engineer. Researcher. Future Physician.

Hello, my name is Kunaal Sarnaik and welcome to my website! Currently, I am in my first year at the Case Western Reserve University School of Medicine studying to become a Doctor of Medicine. Last year, I graduated from the University of Virginia with a Bachelor of Science in biomedical engineering and minors in computer science and data science. I am extremely fascinated with the integration of cutting edge technologies such as machine learning, pattern recognition, image classification, and artificial intelligence in the field of medicine. As such, I hope to spend my life enhancing healthcare quality, outreach, and accuracy on the global scale, attempting to improve patient outcomes along the way.

Outside of school, I have worked as an emergency medicine scribe at the University of Virginia Health System, have participated in several research endeavors and extracurricular societies, and have also pursued the implementation of a novel medical device along with a classmate. I enjoy boxing, reading historical biographies, disassembling electronics, and hiking.

If you have any questions, feel free to reach out!
